
## Class : pubic symphysis

 * *IRI* = http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/UBERON_0003699
 * *Label* = pubic symphysis
 * *Definition* = the firm fibrocartilaginous joint in the median plane between the two opposing surfaces of the pubic bones, which are united by an interpubic disc of fibrocartilage as well as the superior and arcuate pubic ligaments. [ [database_cross_reference](../../ef/oboInOwl#hasDbXref.md) = MP:0009575, [database_cross_reference](../../ef/oboInOwl#hasDbXref.md) = Wikipedia:Pubic_symphysis ]
 * *Comment* = In birds, the pubic symphysis is present only in the ostrich, and the two hip bones are usually widely separated, making it easier to lay large eggs - Romer, Alfred Sherwood; Parsons, Thomas S. (1977). The Vertebrate Body. Philadelphia, PA: Holt-Saunders International. pp. 188Ð192. ISBN 0-03-910284-X.
