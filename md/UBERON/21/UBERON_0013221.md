
## Class : caudofemoralis

 * *IRI* = http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/UBERON_0013221
 * *Label* = caudofemoralis
 * *Definition* = The caudofemoralis (from the latin cauda, tail and femur, thighbone) is a muscle found in the pelvic limb of mostly all animals possessing a tail, since it is a synapomorphy appeared on the Archosauria clade. It is thus found in felids (cats) and Mustela ('Weasels'), but also on crocodiles and birds.
