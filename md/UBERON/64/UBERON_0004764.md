
## Class : intramembranous bone tissue

 * *IRI* = http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/UBERON_0004764
 * *Label* = intramembranous bone tissue
 * *Definition* = Bone tissue that forms directly within mesenchyme, and does not replace other tissues[TAO]. Intramembranous ossification is the formation of bone in which osteoblasts secrete a collagen-proteoglycan matrix that binds calcium salts and becomes calcified[GO]. Intramembranous ossification is the way flat bones and the shell of a turtle are formed[GO]. Unlike endochondral ossification, cartilage is not present during intramembranous ossification[WP].
