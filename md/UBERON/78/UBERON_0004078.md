
## Class : cerebellum vermis lobule IX

 * *IRI* = http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/UBERON_0004078
 * *Label* = cerebellum vermis lobule IX
 * *Definition* = The uvula (uvular lobe) forms a considerable portion of the inferior vermis; it is separated on either side from the tonsil by the sulcus valleculC&, at the bottom of which it is connected to the tonsil by a ridge of gray matter, indented on its surface by shallow furrows, and hence called the furrowed band. [ [database_cross_reference](../../ef/oboInOwl#hasDbXref.md) = http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uvula_of_cerebellum,Wikipedia:Uvula_of_cerebellum ]
 * *Comment* = The uvula is the second largest lobule, following the culmen. It pertains to the paleocerebellum and is separated from the nodule by the posterolateral fissure.
