## Other Annotations

 * *[definition](../../IAO/15/IAO_0000115.md)* = A lobule that is part of a thymus. Divided into an outer cortex and inner medulla and separated from each other by connective tissue septa, but with the medullary tissue continuous from lobule to lobule.[ncit,modified].
 * *[taxon_notes](../../UBPROP/08/UBPROP_0000008.md)* =  in the mouse, the lobes of the thymus are not subdivided into lobules but only in the central medulla and a peripheral cortex (i.e. there is no distinct sublobulation). In humans, the two thymus lobes are composed of many lobules of various sizes which contain follicles, each comprising a medulla and a cortex. in the rat, the thymus is partially subdivided into lobules separated by thin bands of connective tissue which are continuous with the thin connective tissue capsule.[MP]
 * *[structure_notes](../../UBPROP/10/UBPROP_0000010.md)* = Lymphocytes, reticular cells and macrophages are found in the lobules
