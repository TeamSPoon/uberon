
## Class : internodal tract

 * *IRI* = http://purl.obolibrary.org/obo/UBERON_0009966
 * *Label* = internodal tract
 * *Definition* = Subdivision of conducting system of heart which consists of the anterior, middle and  posterior internodal tracts  beneath the endocardium of interatrial septum and above the attachment of the septal leaflet of tricuspid valve. [[database_cross_reference](../../ef/oboInOwl#hasDbXref.md) =  + FMA:9479]
 * *Comment* = evidence is sparse [http://www.gpnotebook.co.uk/simplepage.cfm?ID=-483065798] There is some functional evidence for the existence of specialized conducting pathways within the atria (termed internodal tracts), although this is controversial[http://www.cvphysiology.com/Arrhythmias/A003.htm].
