Terms with changed definitions

VAO:0000001 : surface structure

VAO: Organism subdivision which is the collection of anatomical structures on the body surface.
UBR: Organism subdivision that is the collection of anatomical structures on the body surface[ZFA].

CJM-NOTES:Use VAO

VAO:0000017 : connective tissue

(was portion of connective tissue)

VAO: Tissue with cells that deposit non-polarized extracellular matrix including connective tissue fibres and ground substance.
UBR: Tissue which consists of fibroblasts, osteocytes or chondrocytes and intercellular matrix produced by these cells. Examples: bone (tissue), dense regular elastic tissue, areolar tissue, neuroglia, adipose tissue. // Anatomical entity consisting of tissue that is rich in extracellular matrix (collagen, proteoglycan, etc.) and binds together and is the support of the various structures of the body.

CJM-NOTES: Note that uberon uses terms like "tissue", whereas VAO uses the CARO/FMA-esque "portion of tissue". I would like to retain the simpler form as primary label in Uberon. "portion of connective tissue" is there as a synonym (with synonym provenance to FMA).

CJM-NOTES: Use VAO def but retain some of Uberon def as part of definiton gloss.

VAO:0000025 : dental papilla

(was odontogenic papilla)

VAO: Cell condensation that is a well-defined population of densely organized mesenchymal cells that aggregates adjacent to an epithelium.
UBR: Tissue of the developing tooth. It is composed of pre-odontoblast and undiffentiated mesnchyme cells and connects to the basal lamina[ZFA]. The dental papilla is a condensation of ectomesenchymal cells called odontoblasts, seen in histologic sections of a developing tooth. It lies below a cellular aggregation known as the enamel organ. The dental papilla appears after 8-10 weeks intra uteral life. The dental papilla gives rise to the dentin and pulp of a tooth. The enamel organ, dental papilla, and dental follicle together forms one unit, called the tooth germ. This is of importance because all the tissues of a tooth and its supporting structures form from these distinct cellular aggregations.

CJM-NOTES: VAO uses "odontogenic papilla" instead of "dental papilla". This is a better name to use in a pan-vertebrate context, although there is a case for splitting the class (e.g. dental papilla is an odontogenetic papilla that is part of a developing tooth)

VAO:0000027 : skeletal system

VAO: Multi-element, multi-tissue anatomical cluster.
UBR: Organ system subdivision which consists of the skeleton and the articular system.

CJM-NOTES: Here we start getting into some crucial differences. Uberon has separate classes for "skeleton" and "skeletal system", the former is part of the latter, with classes such as "axial skeleton" part of the skeleton. The skeletal system is divided into the skeleton and articular system (following FMA). Uberon also goes up to "musculoskeletal system", which is skeletal system + muscular system Uberon has the following path for terms such as PC axial skeleton: 

- UBERON:0002204 ! musculoskeletal system
-- UBERON:0001434 ! skeletal system *** 
--- UBERON:0004288 ! skeleton *** 
---- UBERON:0005944 ! axial skeleton *** 
----- UBERON:0002090 ! postcranial axial skeleton ***
This is similar to the FMA.

In contrast, VAO has no term "skeleton". The VAO def of "skeletal system" is under-specified. Also VAO doesn't go up to "musculoskeletal system". 

- VAO:0000027 ! skeletal system
-- VAO:0000035 ! dermal skeleton *** 
--- VAO:0000090 ! integumentary skeleton *** 
-- VAO:0000037 ! endoskeleton *** 
-- VAO:0000056 ! axial skeleton *** 
--- VAO:0000049 ! cranial skeleton *** 
--- VAO:0000093 ! postcranial axial skeleton *** 
-- VAO:0000076 ! appendicular skeleton ***

Recommendation: adopt the FMA/uberon distinction. Some work migh need done on the articular system and how this relates to cartilaginous skeletons (note VAO doesn't have any terms for joints or articular elements).

VAO:0000029 : integument

VAO: Surface structure that is the outer protective covering of the body.
UBR: The dermis, epidermis and hypodermis.

CJM-NOTES:The uberon definition is more specific, but might it be too restrictive? The uberon def should be refined anyway to make it clear this is a mereological sum, not class union.

VAO:0000032 : notochord

VAO: Avascular multi-tissue structure composed of large vacuolated epithelial cells (chordablasts) and perichordal fibrous tissue.
UBR: The notochord is an elongate, rod-like, skeletal structure dorsal to the gut tube and ventral to the nerve cord. The notochord should not be confused with the backbone or vertebral column of most adult vertebrates. The notochord appears early in embryogeny and plays an important role in promoting or organizing the embryonic development of nearby structures. In most adult chordates the notochord disappears or becomes highly modified. In some non-vertebrate chordates and fishes the notochord persists as a laterally flexible but incompressible skeletal rod that prevents telescopic collapse of the body during swimming[TOLWEB]. a flexible, rod-shaped body found in embryos of all chordates. It is composed of cells derived from the mesoderm and defines the primitive axis of the embryo. In some chordates, it persists throughout life as the main axial support of the body, while in most vertebrates it becomes the vertebral body of the vertebral column. The notochord is found ventral to the neural tube. The notochord is composed primarily of a core of glycoproteins, encased in a sheath of collagen fibers wound into two opposing helices. The angle between these fibers determines whether increased pressure in the core will result in shortening and thickening versus lengthening and thinning[WP].

CJM-NOTES:Use VAO def. Retain portion of UBR def as gloss?

VAO:0000036 : perichondrium

VAO: Fibrous connective tissue that surrounds cartilage.
UBR: Portion of connective tissue which surrounds cartilage of developing bone.

CJM-NOTES:Use VAO

VAO:0000040 : cartilage

(was cartilage tissue)

VAO: Skeletal tissue that is avascular, rich in glycosaminoglycans (GAGs) and typically includes chondrocytes within isolated lacunae. Cartilage tissue is deposited by chondroblasts.
UBR: A portion of connective tissue dominated by extracellular matrix containing collagen type II and large amounts of proteoglycan, particularly chondroitin sulfate[GO]. Regular connective tissue, which consists of chondrocytes and related cells, the intercellular matrix of which is chondrified. Examples: hyaline cartilage, fibrocartilage, elastic cartilage[FMA]. an avascular supporting and articular skeletal tissue. It also functions as the primary endoskeletal support in vertebrate embryos. Cartilage is deposited by and is composed of chondroblasts and chondrocytes separated by an extracellular matrix, which may or may not mineralize depending on cartilage type, age, or taxon[Hall and Witten].

CJM-NOTES:Use VAO (also change the GO def to reflect VAO)

VAO:0000047 : bone tissue

VAO: Skeletal tissue with a collagen-rich extracellular matrix vascularized, mineralized with hydroxyapatite and typically including osteocytes located in lacunae that communicate with one another by cell processes (in canaliculi). Bone is deposited by osteoblasts.
UBR: The major structural and supportive connective tissue of the body. Osseous tissue forms the rigid part of the bone organs that make up the skeletal system.

CJM-NOTES:Use VAO

VAO:0000056 : axial skeleton

VAO: Anatomical cluster of the central body axis including the cranium, vertebrae, notochord, ribs, and sternum.
UBR: Subdivision of skeleton which consists of skull, set of all vertebrae, set of all ribs and sternum. Examples: There is only one axial skeleton.

CJM-NOTES:See comments on postcranial.

VAO:0000062 : cementum

VAO: Odontoid tissue that is deposited by cementoblasts onto dentine tissue and functions to attach teeth, odontodes and other odontogenic derivatives to bone tissue and the integument.
UBR: A bone-like mineralized tissue secreted by cementoblasts on the surface of root dentine or, in some animals, crown enamel[Diekwisch 2001]. Substance of tooth produced by cementoblasts; surrounds the dentine of the root of the tooth[FMA]. a specialized calcified substance covering the root of a tooth. Cementum is excreted by cells called cementoblasts within the root of the tooth and is thickest at the root apex. Its coloration is yellowish and it is softer than enamel and dentin due to being less mineralized[WP].

CJM-NOTES:Use VAO

VAO:0000065 : enamel

VAO: Skeletal tissue that consists of an avascular, noncollagenous, hypermineralized matrix that is deposited by ameloblasts that are excluded from the matrix. Enamel develops from organic pre-enamel tissue that is replaced by a largely inorganic matrix.
UBR: A dentine-like hypermineralized substance that covers the tooth tip. Enamel's primary mineral is hydroxylapatite, which is a crystalline calcium phosphate. Unlike dentin and bone, enamel does not contain collagen. Instead, it has two unique classes of proteins called amelogenins and enamelins[WP].

CJM-NOTES:Use VAO

VAO:0000069 : dentine

VAO: Collagen-rich odontogenic tissue characteristic of teeth and tooth-like skeletal elements (e.g., odontodes); mature dentine is mineralized and develops from predentine tissue; often (but not always) tubular and acellular.
UBR: a tubular mineralized tissue[H&W]. In mammals - substance of tooth produced by odontoblasts; surrounds the pulp of the tooth and is subjacent to enamel and cement[FMA]. Mineralized collagen containing substance that forms the tooth structure[ZFA]. a calcified tissue of the body, and along with enamel, cementum, and pulp is one of the four major components of teeth[WP].

CJM-NOTES:Use VAO

VAO:0000072 : ligament

VAO: Dense regular connective tissue connecting two or more adjacent skeletal elements or supporting an organ.
UBR: Short band of tough fibrous dense regular connective tissue composed mainly of long, stringy collagen fibers. Ligaments connect bones to other bones to form a joint.

CJM-NOTES:Use VAO

VAO:0000073 : tendon

VAO: Dense regular connective tissue that connects muscle to bone.
UBR: A fibrous, strong, connective tissue that connects muscle to bone or integument and is capable of withstanding tension. Tendons are similar to ligaments except that ligaments join one bone to another. Tendons and muscles work together and can only exert a pulling force.

CJM-NOTES:Use VAO

VAO:0000076 : appendicular skeleton

VAO: Anatomical cluster consisting of the paired appendages (limbs or fins).
UBR: Subdivision of skeleton which consists of the bones of the upper and lower limbs/appendages.

CJM-NOTES:The VAO definition is incorrect, the appendicular skeleton has neither limbs nor fins as parts - it has the limb or fin skeleton/bones as part. Recommendation: merge defs

VAO:0000079 : dermal denticle

(was odontode scale)

VAO: Multi-tissue structure composed of enameloid, dentine surrounding a pulp cavity, and bone of attachment anchoring the element into the dermis.
UBR: small outgrowths which cover the skin of many cartilaginous fish including sharks. They are similar in structure to teeth, and teeth may have evolved from denticle-like structures in primitive fish.[citation needed] The placoid scales in sharks are formed of dentine with dermal papillae located in the core. These scales develop from the dermal layer of the skin, which is in contrast to the epidermal development seen the in the elasmoid scales of tetrapods. The shape of denticle varies from species to species and can be used in identification. Studies have found that the denticles create tiny vortices that reduce drag to make swimming more efficient. Denticles also allow sharks to swim silently compared to other fish that generate considerable noise when they ply the water.

CJM-NOTES:Use VAO name and def.

VAO:0000091 : elastic cartilage

(was elastic cartilage tissue)

VAO: Cartilage tissue that is flexible and contains abundant elastic fibers.
UBR: Cartilage (tissue) which consists of chondrocytes and collagen, the intercellular matrix of which consists of elastic fibers.

CJM-NOTES:Happy to use VAO name and def here, but the FMA-sourced def seems more specific (too specific?). Was this considered and explicitly rejected as the meeting? If so it would be good to record the reasons why as metadata in the ontology.

VAO:0000093 : postcranial axial skeleton

VAO: Skeletal system of the central body axis including vertebrae, notochord, ribs, and sternum.
UBR: The post-cranial subdivision of skeleton structural components forming the long axis of the vertebrate body; in Danio, consisting of the notochord, vertebrae, ribs, supraneurals, intermuscular bones, and unpaired median fins; in human consists of the bones of the vertebral column, the thoracic cage and the pelvis[ZFA+FMA].

CJM-NOTES:Both definitions need work. The VAO genus is "skeletal system", not clear if this is correct, see notes on skeleton above. Both definitions use an undefined term ("central body axis" / "long axis"). Both definitions are underspecified (e.g. nothing in the VAO def says that the pelvis is included). I think a better definition would simply be something like "the part of the axial skeleton after and not including the cranium".

(was hyaline cartilage tissue)

VAO: Cartilage tissue primarily composed of type II collagen (thin fibrils) and a glassy appearance.
UBR: Cartilage (tissue) which consists of chondrocytes and collagen, the intercellular matrix of which is an amorphous gel with a perichondrium.

CJM-NOTES: Happy to reject the FMA-sourced UBR definition, but I wonder if the FMA def was explicitly considered and rejected, and if so why?

VAO:0000103 : fibrocartilage

VAO: Cartilage tissue intermediate between hyaline cartilage and dense connective tissue; contains of abundant collagen fibers; chondrocytes often arranged in columns.
UBR: a mixture of white fibrous tissue and cartilaginous tissue in various proportions. It owes its flexibility and toughness to the former of these constituents, and its elasticity to the latter. It is the only type of cartilage that contains type I collagen in addition to the normal type II[WP]. Cartilage (tissue) which consists of chondrocytes and collagen, the intercellular matrix of which is an amorphous gel without a well-defined perichondrium[FMA].

CJM-NOTES: Happy to reject the FMA-sourced UBR definition, but I wonder if the FMA def was explicitly considered and rejected, and if so why?

VAO:0000128 : skeletal element

VAO: Organ entity that may have different tissue compositions at different stages and typically involved in mechanical support.
UBR: Organ consisting of skeletal tissue. Encompasses whole bones, fused bones, cartilaginious elements, teeth, dermal denticles.

CJM-NOTES: The VAO def uses "organ entity" as a genus, but this isn't defined elsewhere. Why not make use of the term "skeletal tissue", which is already defined in VAO? This would make the definition stronger. Also seems like a good principle to define a structure by its composition at a lower level of granularity, and so on, recursively.

VAO:0000130 : dermal skeletal element

(was dermal bone)

VAO: Skeletal element that forms superficially in the organism, usually in association with the ectoderm.
UBR: dermis-derived entity that is made of skeletal tissue.

CJM-NOTES: I presume the expert-vetted VAO definition is the better one here. Can we be more specific than "superficially"? Qualifiers like "usually" are better kept in the definition gloss if possible. Can we reference the GO process in the definition here. Also, there's nothing in the definition saying this is a bone; in fact one major difference between VAO and UBR is that VAO has no term "bone" - it lumps bone (organ) and bone tissue together. See Fig 2 of my pre-skeletal meeting white paper for more details here. Uberon is likely to keep the distinction between bone and bone tissue.

VAO:0000145 : endochondral bone

VAO: Replacement bone that forms within cartilage.
UBR: Replacement bone that forms within cartilage[TAO]. Endochondral ossification is the formation of bone by the replacement of cartilage tissue with mineralized bone[GO]. cartilage is present during endochondral ossification. It is also an essential process during the rudimentary formation of long bones, the growth of the length of long bones, and the natural healing of bone fractures[WP].

CJM-NOTES: Defs are identical, UBR has additional gloss.
